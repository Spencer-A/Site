Citizenship

How can I be a good citizen?

Citizenship: "the quality of an individual's response to membership in a community" [1]

We are all part of several communities, and it is our job to serve those communites through citizenship. However, being a good citizen-consistently doing what is best for the community-is sometimes much easier than other times. Thus, the true test of citizenship comes when it is hard, not when it is easy. Thus, many traits are necessary to be a good ciitzen, such as:

  • Honesty: Conducting yourself in a fair, sincere manner. To be deceitful is almost always done to cause harm of some kind, and thus when a member of a community is dishonest, it negatively impacts said entire community
  • Compassion: Being sensitive and attentive to other peopleâ€™s struggles. In a communinity it is necessary to care for those that are not well, and that often requires sympathy regarding how others are struggling
  • Respect: Having a fundamental appreciation for people just because they are human beings. Communities are diverse groups, and without basic respect for everyone in it the community will splinter and suffer
  • Responsibility: Accountability for your own self. You occupy a specific place in a community, and you must be trustworthy and accountable to successfully fulfill that role
  • Courage: Willingness to put yourself in uncomfortable situations. This attribute is necessary in citizenship because if you are only willing to be a citizen during comfortable times, that is not true citizenship
  • Selflessness: Putting the well-being of others above your own comfort (to a reasonable degree). If you want to consistently do what is best for the community, that will often require you to put its needs and wellbeing over your own comfort

Recently, I have been following the actions of the American Civil Liberties Union (ACLU). For decades, this organization has been a principled group actively practicing their citizenship in the community that in the United States. I am constantly inspired by their role in actively fighting for American democracy and their characteristics of honesty, compassion, respect, responsibility, courage, and selflessness
